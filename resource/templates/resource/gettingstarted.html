LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is a method that allows you to fine-tune large models efficiently by introducing small trainable matrices while keeping the main model weights frozen. This means you can train customized models with minimal compute and storage â€” ideal for personal or small-scale use cases.

The compactness also allows LoRAs to be shared very easily in a plug-and-play fashion. Note, however, that a LoRA can only be used on the corresponding base model it was originally trained from.

This website is a place for people to catalouge and share(through the "Borrow" feature) the LoRA's they have trained. For resources on how to train your own LoRA, check out the "LoRA Resources" section of the site.
